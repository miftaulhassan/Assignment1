A tradition of feasting: 10 Vietnamese Tet meals

At Tet, a diverse array of dishes from sticky rice cakes and spring rolls to boiled chicken graces family food trays everywhere, symbolizing prosperity, luck, and familial unity. As the Lunar New Year approaches, Vietnamese households bustle with excitement and anticipation, preparing for one of the most significant and cherished celebrations of the year. Join us on a culinary journey as we explore the 10 common dishes that adorn the Lunar New Year food tray in Vietnam.

1. Banh chung/banh tet (sticky rice cakes)

Banh chung, a square rice cake, is commonly enjoyed savory in northern Vietnam, while banh tet, a cylindrical sticky rice cake and a specialty of central and southern region, can be found in both savory and sweet variations.Once cut, banh chung reveals a contrasting light color against its dark green exterior (a product of the banana or dong leaves it's wrapped in).
